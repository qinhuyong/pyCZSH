# Atomistic model construction and quasi-static mechanical workflow

Atomistic calcium-silicate-hydrate (C-S-H) models were generated using a
pyCSH brick-based C-S-H construction strategy. The workflow was extended to
introduce Zn-containing local motifs while preserving CementFF4/CementFF4-Zn
compatible atom typing, charge assignment, bonded topology, water topology, and
core-shell metadata in the exported LAMMPS data files.

Zn-modified C-S-H candidates were constructed as static structural candidates,
not as experimentally unique or fully equilibrated models. Two Zn local
environments were considered. The Q2b_Zn motif corresponds to Zn substitution at
a Q(2b)-type bridging silicate site, representing a Q(2p,Zn)-related local
environment. The Q1_Zn motif corresponds to Zn substitution at a Q(1)-type
terminal silicate site, representing a Q(1,Zn)-related local environment. In
both cases, the local substitution was charge-balanced using a ZnO2(OH)2-style
motif. Safe terminal or non-bridging oxygen core-shell pairs were converted to
Oh-Hoh pairs where required for charge balance, while unsafe bridging oxygens
were not hydroxylated by default.

Zn was assigned charge +2 and the CementFF4-Zn Zn atom type. The generated data
files used the CementFF4-Zn compatible type mapping and force-field include
files. Zn-centered angle types were remapped consistently for O-Zn-O,
Oh-Zn-O, Oh-Zn-Oh, and Zn-Oh-H local geometries. Harmonic Zn-O bonds were not
introduced. Each generated structure was accompanied by JSON summaries of Zn
site selection, motif construction, charge balance, water topology, CementFF4
mapping, and validation results.

Every candidate was subjected to an initial static validation step. The
validator checked total charge residual, per-atom charge assignment, atom, bond,
and angle type validity, CS-Info consistency, water topology, core-shell
pairing, and Zn-O coordination. Candidate structures were then passed through
LAMMPS read-check, run0, and static minimization steps when requested. Because
LAMMPS write_data does not preserve the custom CS-Info section, CS-Info entries
were reattached by atom ID before post-minimization validation.

Single-Zn ensembles were generated from independent random seeds and included
Q1_Zn, Q2b_Zn, or ensemble-level Q1/Q2b mixtures. Multi-Zn structures were
generated by placing multiple independent Zn motifs into the same C-S-H parent
structure. The multi-Zn workflow supported multi-Q1_Zn, multi-Q2b_Zn, and
single-structure Q1_Zn + Q2b_Zn mixed-motif candidates. This mixed-motif
definition is distinct from the old mixed_Q1_Q2b_Zn site type, which was not
enabled. For multi-Zn candidates, each Zn center was recorded independently,
including substituted Si site, motif type, selected oxygen atoms, hydroxylated
oxygen pairs, Zn-Zn separation, and pre- and post-minimization Zn-O
coordination.

Post-minimized multi-Zn candidates were classified using per-center Zn-O
coordination diagnostics. A minimum-valid multi-Zn candidate required every Zn
center to retain at least four O neighbors within the unchanged 2.5 Angstrom
Zn-O validation gate. Coordination quality was then reported separately:
ideal_fourfold indicates that all Zn centers had coordination exactly 4;
overcoordinated indicates that all Zn centers had coordination at least 4 and at
least one center had coordination greater than 4; undercoordinated_failed
indicates that at least one Zn center had coordination less than 4.
Overcoordinated models were treated as minimum-valid candidates but were not
described as ideal ZnO4 tetrahedral structures.

For quasi-static mechanical workflow validation, selected post-minimized
structures were subjected to independent x-direction strain cases with
prescribed strains of -0.003, -0.002, -0.001, +0.001, +0.002, and +0.003. Each
strain case started from the same post-minimized reference structure; no
sequential strain accumulation was used. For each strain case, the simulation
cell was deformed, statically minimized, written to a LAMMPS data file,
reattached with CS-Info, and revalidated. The actual strain was computed from
the final and initial x box lengths as final_lx / initial_lx - 1. Stress and
pressure values parsed from LAMMPS thermo output were reported in bar and
converted to GPa using 1 bar = 1.0e-4 GPa.

The workflow described here is a static candidate generation, validation,
screening, and quasi-static mechanics pipeline. It is not a finite-temperature
MD protocol, does not report final elastic constants, does not constitute a
production mechanical-property workflow, and does not assign an MD-ready label
to any generated structure.
